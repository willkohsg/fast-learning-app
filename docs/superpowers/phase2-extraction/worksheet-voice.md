## 3. How William Frames Error Logging

### The Opening Pitch (from WordForWord.pdf)

> **"The Power of Error Logging in Maths: Turning Mistakes into Marks"**

> "Everyone makes mistakes in maths. The problem isn't the mistake itself... It's not knowing why we made it. Error logging helps us learn from mistakes instead of repeating them."

**Tone:** Framing error logging as a *learning tool*, not punishment. Emphasizes diagnosis over blame.

### The Why It Works (Mechanism)

> "**Builds awareness** — 'I always make this kind of mistake.'  
> **Creates patterns** — we see where we go wrong most often.  
> **Directs practice** — instead of repeating random questions, we target weak spots."

**Tone:** Systems thinking. Shifts student mindset from "I'm bad at maths" to "I have a specific pattern I can fix."

### The Role of Students & Parents

> "**Student:** Takes ownership — 'These are my errors, and here's how I'll fix them.'  
> **Tutors:** Analyse error patterns — customise lessons.  
> **Parents:** See progress transparently through logs."

**Tone:** Shared responsibility. Empowers all three stakeholders. Emphasizes transparency.

### The Improvement Cycle (Framed as Virtue)

> "**The Improvement Cycle:**  
> 1. Attempt paper  
> 2. Log errors  
> 3. Analyse patterns  
> 4. Revise & drill  
> 5. Retest"

**Tone:** Systematic, not mysterious. Students understand *why* each step matters.

### The Closing (Reframe)

> "Mistakes are not failures. Every error is feedback. With error logging: Less guessing. More targeted improvement."

**Tone:** Emotional permission + practical outcome. Reframes struggle as progress.

## 4. Voice Patterns in Worksheet Context

### Structural Patterns

1. **The "What Not, But What" Move:**
   - "The problem isn't the mistake itself... It's not knowing why we made it."
   - This is William's diagnostic signature: name the surface symptom, then reveal the *real* problem.

2. **The Action-to-Insight Flow:**
   - List action (Attempt paper → Log errors → Analyse → Revise → Retest)
   - Then extract the insight ("we see where we go wrong most often")
   - Students understand system *and* benefit simultaneously.

3. **The Ownership Shift:**
   - Shifts from "tutors teach" to "students diagnose themselves"
   - "Takes ownership — 'These are my errors, and here's how I'll fix them.'"
   - Classic William move: identity > mechanics.

### Lexical Patterns

- **"Instead of..."** — Contrasts unfocused effort with targeted practice
  - "instead of repeating random questions, we target weak spots"
- **"The problem isn't X, it's Y"** — Diagnostic distinction
- **"This is feedback"** — Reframe. Mistakes → data.
- **Ownership language:** "takes ownership", "my errors", "here's how I'll fix them"
- **Process language:** "analyse", "target", "pattern", "directed"

## 6. Open Questions for William

1. **"Did not complete working":** Is this primarily a T-pillar issue (student ran out of time), or an S-pillar issue (student didn't know the steps to show)? Or both?

2. **"Time management issue" as Error Type:** This appears as both an error *type* (Section A) and error *category* (deeper cause under "Poor exam strategy"). Should they be separated or clarified?

3. **Anxiety/Panic:** The worksheet lists "Anxiety/panic" as an error category, but the script doesn't detail how to diagnose it. Is this emotional scaffolding part of the T pillar, or a separate dimension?

4. **"Confusion between similar concepts":** Which pillar is primary — S (pattern library missing) or F (concept not fully understood)? When mapping this error category to a pillar, what's your hierarchy?

5. **Careless errors (sign, arithmetic, copying):** In practice, do students recognize these as "T pillar" (rushing due to time pressure), or do you have a separate protocol for distinguishing "I made a silly mistake" from "I don't understand"?

6. **Script vs. Worksheet:** The WordForWord script defines the 6 error *types* briefly. Are there deeper William-specific explanations (beyond what's in the script) that belong in the student-facing diagnostic tool, or is the script the authoritative voice?
